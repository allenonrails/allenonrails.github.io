Will you be mine?

You’re the whispering wind through the walls soft and thin,
The glow of the streetlights in silver night’s sheen.
Without you, the stars fade away in the sky,
Like echoes that vanish, no matter how high. You’re the melody spun in the morning’s cool air,
The laughter that dances with raindrops so rare.
Without you, time lingers, then stumbles, then sways,
As if it’s forgotten the count of its days. But soon, I just know, you’ll be back here again,
And warmth will return to our world once the same.
Sonya, my Valentine — will you be mine?
I wait. And I count to a million in time.
